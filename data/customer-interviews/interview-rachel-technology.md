# Customer Interview Transcript
**Date:** February 8, 2026
**Customer:** Rachel Park, Head of Operations at InnovateTech Solutions
**Interviewer:** Product Team
**Duration:** 42 minutes

## Interview Transcript

**Interviewer:** Rachel, thanks for joining us. Could you describe your position and how you're currently leveraging our platform?

**Rachel:** Of course! I lead operations at InnovateTech - been in this role for nearly four years. We started using your platform about ten months ago, focusing mainly on client onboarding sequences and internal authorization workflows.

**Interviewer:** How would you characterize the experience so far?

**Rachel:** Overall quite positive. The platform has helped us bring consistency to our processes. Previously everything was ad-hoc - endless email threads, spreadsheet tracking, tasks slipping through gaps. Now we have much better structure.

**Interviewer:** What tangible improvements have you observed?

**Rachel:** Our client onboarding cycle dropped from nearly three weeks down to about six days. That's transformative for us. The automatic follow-ups and task delegation mean nothing falls off our radar anymore. We used to have clients waiting indefinitely because someone overlooked creating their system access.

**Interviewer:** Have you encountered any obstacles or pain points?

**Rachel:** Definitely. The most significant is mobile accessibility. Our field personnel essentially can't use the platform when they're deployed. They either have to wait until returning to the office or phone someone to handle updates remotely. Major productivity drain.

**Interviewer:** Can you illustrate with a real scenario?

**Rachel:** Just last week, one of our field engineers was on-site with a customer and discovered our configured workflow didn't match their particular infrastructure. He had to call me, describe the situation over the phone while I made real-time adjustments on my desktop. Burned 40 minutes on something that should've taken maybe 7 minutes.

**Interviewer:** That's clearly frustrating. Other challenges?

**Rachel:** The onboarding curve for new team members is surprisingly steep. We've brought on seven people in recent months, and they need at least four weeks before they're comfortable building workflows independently. I keep thinking there should be starter templates or something to accelerate that learning.

**Interviewer:** Expand on that thought.

**Rachel:** When someone joins the team, I spend hours walking them through workflow creation fundamentals. The thing is, probably 85% of our workflows follow predictable patterns. Approval flows, for instance - they all have the same skeleton: submission, review stage, approve or decline, notification. Yet everyone reconstructs it from scratch each time.

**Interviewer:** Have you explored alternative solutions?

**Rachel:** We assessed FlowBuilder Pro and Automation Hub. FlowBuilder has excellent templates but limited integration capabilities. Automation Hub is incredibly powerful but overwhelming for our team's skill level. Your platform strikes a good balance - just needs those usability refinements.

**Interviewer:** If you could add three capabilities immediately, what would you choose?

**Rachel:** Easy choice - native mobile application, ready-to-use workflow templates, and robust analytics. Oh, and if I get a bonus fourth, some kind of intelligent assistant that recommends workflow optimizations. We're likely not maximizing the platform's capabilities.

**Interviewer:** Tell me more about the analytics gap.

**Rachel:** Currently I access basic data like completion percentages and duration metrics. What I actually need is insight generation: which process steps create delays? Where do workflows typically stall? Are there failure patterns we can address? I need intelligence, not just raw numbers.

**Interviewer:** How are you measuring success with the platform today?

**Rachel:** Pretty manually, to be honest. I export to Excel monthly and build custom reports. Tracking weekly workflow volume, average completion times, error frequency. Takes me several hours every month to compile.

**Interviewer:** What's the business cost of these limitations?

**Rachel:** The mobile gap alone probably wastes 12-18 hours weekly across the team. Template absence means new hires take a month to reach productivity instead of maybe ten days. Without proper analytics, I'm definitely missing optimization opportunities. Conservative estimate: we're leaving 25-35% of potential efficiency gains unrealized.

**Interviewer:** How does your team generally feel about the platform?

**Rachel:** Mixed reception. Office-based team members love it - massive upgrade from our previous manual approach. Field teams tolerate it but resent the mobile constraints. New employees find it initially daunting but appreciate it after training.

**Interviewer:** Any final reflections or suggestions?

**Rachel:** Bottom line, your platform has been valuable for us. It's addressed our core challenges around standardization and automation. But unlocking its complete potential requires those mobile capabilities and better onboarding resources. Deliver on those, and this becomes truly game-changing for companies in our space.

**Interviewer:** This has been extremely valuable, Rachel. Thanks for your candor and time.

**Rachel:** My pleasure! Really excited about the platform's trajectory. Don't hesitate to contact me for follow-up questions or additional feedback.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Mobile Accessibility Gap:** Field personnel are restricted by the lack of a native mobile app, leading to productivity loss and reliance on office-based staff for real-time adjustments.
2. **Steep Onboarding Curve:** New hires take ~4 weeks to become proficient; "starter templates" for common patterns (like approval flows) would accelerate time-to-productivity to ~10 days.
3. **From Data to Insights:** There is a high demand for "insight generation" (identifying process bottlenecks and failure patterns) rather than just raw completion metrics.
4. **Significant Efficiency Gains:** Despite limitations, the platform reduced the client onboarding cycle from 3 weeks to 6 days by introducing structure and automatic follow-ups.
5. **Pattern-Based Building:** 85% of workflows follow predictable skeletons; implementing a template library would eliminate redundant "from scratch" development.
